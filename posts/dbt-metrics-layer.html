dbt & the metrics layer

Drew Banin (dbt's CPO) raised an issue on Github where he explains why it makes sense for dbt to be able to define metrics and shares what the first version of this could look like.

I'm not going to expand further on what Drew wrote about or even on what the metrics layer is, as the issue itself gives an amazing overview of that and points you to further resources to understand it.

I believe this is a super interesting move for dbt to take.

As Benn wrote, the modern data stack has consisted on tools focusing on horizontal areas of the data process. dbt has positioned itself in the transformation layer from the very beginning, and it has been the stronger player on that area for a while now.

However, dbt is not just a transformation tool. In fact, I believe the moment they released the documentation capabilities of dbt + dbt docs, they implicitly recognized that themselves.
